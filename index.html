Where we stay

Eight nights in Châtel, one airport night in Ferney-Voltaire.

Sat 11 → Sun 19 · Chalet “Les Erines”

Châtel village centre, France 4 bedrooms · 9 beds · 4.5 baths

  • Booked by Eevert on Airbnb (listing) — host Robert, rated 4.87★. Coordinates ≈ 46.2647 N, 6.8383 E · open in Google Maps.
  • Village-centre location: Super Châtel gondola (stage 6) and the SOW shuttle stop are a short walk; “kisapaikat tosi lähellä”, as Äiti put it.
  • Heads-up from the host: a construction site sits directly behind the chalet — workdays may run 07:30–19:00. With 09:00+ starts it shouldn’t bite, but don’t plan on sleeping in.
  • Châtel is at ~1 200 m: warm days, cool evenings, occasional big thunderstorms.

Sun 19 → Mon 20 · Zenitude Swiss Appart Hotel

13 chemin du Levant, 01210 Ferney-Voltaire, France 5 min from GVA

  • Booked by Alma for 7 people, 2 apartments (each: 1 double bed + sofa bed), breakfast included (buffet from 07:30 weekends, 06:30 weekdays), ~€201 + €106 breakfast, pay at the hotel.
  • Check-in from 15:00 · checkout by 11:00 · reception 24 h · bring ID + card for the €200 deposit.
  • Free airport shuttle hotel→GVA: Mon–Fri 07:00–10:00 (Sat–Sun 08:00–11:00) — ideal for Monday-morning flights. One direction only (to the airport). Phone +33 4 50 99 51 51.
  • Free outdoor parking · pool 09:30–21:00 · to the hotel by public transport: bus 66 from the airport or bus F from Cornavin.

Getting around

Two rental cars are booked; the third-car-or-bus question for your four is to-do #1.

Cars

  • Eevert & Venla: compact, 11–19.7 (Budget via Auto Europe, no extra insurance taken).
  • Alma & Markus: small car, 11–20.7.
  • Your four: nothing booked. Chat idea on record: ride out with Eevert, back with Alma; or keep Eevert’s car one day longer with shared driving on Sun 19.7.
  • Village parking is mostly free; stage valley-station car parks are paid, on site.

SOW bus (adults born ≤2005)

  • Morgins⇄Châtel shuttle: CHF 10/day · CHF 50/week. Runs check-in day, every race day and prize-giving evenings, at least hourly.
  • Full stage-bus pass (also serves E2, E3, E4, E5 arenas): CHF 40/day · CHF 60/week.
  • Buy in the trackmaxx webshop — can be added after registration; day tickets added 3 July.
  • Swiss travelcards (GA etc.) are not valid on these buses.

Lifts & borders

  • All race-day mountain lifts are included in the entry fee for week entrants — gondolas and chairlifts per stage, no tickets needed.
  • Non-competing companions pay normal lift rates (no companion pass exists).
  • Border hops (Pas de Morgins) are unmanned Schengen crossings — but carry ID always.
  • No Swiss vignette needed for Châtel life via France; needed only for the A9 motorway approach.

Viivi → Geneva Airport, Fri 17 July

The constraint: she flies home on Friday while everyone else races E5 at Avoriaz. The luck: every family start is 12:54–12:58, so the whole morning is free.

Recommended

Drop at Thonon + Léman Express

~€15 · door → GVA ≈ 2 h 15

  • One driver does Châtel⇄Thonon (~35–40 min each way, all in France, no Geneva traffic).
  • Trains roughly twice hourly from ~05:30; change at Genève-Cornavin, then 2 stops (6 min) to Genève-Aéroport.
  • Aim to land at GVA ~2.5 h before her flight. Book on the SBB app / SNCF Connect once her flight time is confirmed.
Zero driving

Bus 12 + train

~€20 · ≈ 2 h 30–3 h

  • EVAD line 12 Châtel → Thonon gare (~1 h; early departure ≈ 06:10, few runs per day), then the same train.
  • Weekday timetable applies on Friday — but verify the exact departure with the tourist office (+33 4 50 73 22 44) or evad.fr.
  • Cheapest, but the sparse bus is the weak link.
Fallback

Private transfer

€240–270 / car · ≈ 1 h 30 door-to-door

  • Chatel Transfer (local): +33 7 87 04 21 45, info@chateltransfer.com — private only in summer.
  • Ski-Lifts from ~€240/vehicle; shared summer seats from Châtel are effectively unavailable.
  • Book ahead if no one can drive at all.

Race kit

  • O-suit, O-shoes (rocky alpine slopes — studs fine, spikes overkill), compass, gaiters.
  • Rain shell + long trousers in the race bag every day — the organiser can mandate full-cover clothing at start in bad weather (Competition Rules art. 11).
  • SI cards per to-do #4; bibs from check-in (keep them all week).
  • Warm layer + dry change for arenas at 1 800–2 000 m — chairlift rides down get cold in rain.

Everything else

  • Passports/ID (daily border hops), EHIC cards, travel insurance details.
  • Some CHF cash/card for stage parking and Morgins; euros for Châtel.
  • Sunscreen + cap — 2 000 m sun between the showers is fierce.
  • Pool gear: chalet is near Lac de Vonnes; Zenitude has a pool (towels not provided!).
  • Trekking poles only if someone walks a Holiday class — banned elsewhere.
